Or, more specifically the page where you are able to download the chrome extension I created as my Thesis. Please click here to download it. Instructions for how to install the extension are at the bottom of this page.

This chrome extension uses HTTP cookies installed on user's computers by the websites they visit and Tone.js to create a dynamically generated soundscape.

"What are HTTP cookies? Why should I care about them? And what is a soundscape?" are all questions I expect you might have and I will do my best to answer them in brief. Should you want a much longer winded explanation please click here and you can read the written portion of my thesis in its entirety.

HTTP cookies are one of the building blocks of the modern internet. Cookies come to us from a time when computing memory was much more expensive than it is today. They were used as a way to assist customers in keeping track of what was in their e-commerce shopping carts.

Today the purpose of cookies has been almost completely inverted. Rather than helping people keep track of things they might like to buy, cookies have turned people into products tech companies might like to buy. The product sold is the data collected by cookies about user's browsing habits.

As of now there is very little notification for users as to the purpose of the cookies being installed on their computers. The sheer number installed on a user's computer during an average browsing session can be staggering, with individual sites installing hundreds at a time. The aim of this extension is to use sound to alert users about the number, and some of the properties of the cookies being installed on user's computers.

The soundscape created is tied to four different attributes of cookies. The lowest frequency is played for one second per cookie. The frequency of the percussive bass note is controlled by the security status, and domain associated with each cookie. The panning of the middle frequency is controlled by whether a cookie is a session cookie or not. Session cookies are panned to the right and non-session cookies are panned to the left. The highest note is controlled by the differnce between cookies that are short lived, and long lived. Cookies I considered to be short lived last on a user's computer for less than one week Cookies considered to be long lived remain longer than one week.

If you would like to know more about the technical parameters of cookies this is an excellent place to start.

Instructions for installation:
  1. Download the extension by clicking here.
  2. The file you downloaded is compressed, so it must be decompressed. Double click on the file to do so. (The file most likely was placed in your downloads folder after it finished downloading, in case you can't find it.)
  3. Now, open a new tab in chrome and type, chrome://extensions into chrome's navigation bar.
  4. In the upper right hand corner toggle the slider "Developer Mode".
  5. In the upper lefthand corner click the button that says "Load unpacked".
  6. Click once on the uncompressed extension. Click "select".
  7. In the upper right hand corner of your browser there will be an icon with the letter 'A'. Quit your browser, restart it, and click the 'A' icon.
  8. Enjoy!
