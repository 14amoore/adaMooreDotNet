Ideas In Form 3D to 4D

These two pieces are the final steps in a project created for a prompt called "Ideas In Form". We were to take an idea from 1D (writing/drawing), to 2D (web design), to 3D (physical object), to 4D (a piece incorporating an element related to time).

The domain I chose to explore for this series of pieces was graffiti. I wanted to repurpose screens placed in public subway stations and use them to ask questions rather than exhort people to buy more things. I also felt it was important when making these pieces to do no damage to the installation site. By not damaging the installation site people would be free to ponder the questions being posed, instead of being distracted by the damage being done by their installation.

To create the 3D piece I made three collapsible wooden frames and stenciled the words "Is" "This" "Ours" onto them so they would form a question when installed on the displays. This was done in the hopes that at least a few people passing would question who has the right to use public space and for what purposes. For the 4D piece I incorporated time by using an arduino, a PIR sensor, and a stepper motor so that the words on the frames would recombine into new questions as people passed them by.
